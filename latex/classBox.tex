Calculate the force on a particle exerted by all other particles. 

Uses /f\$ \textbackslash{}mathbf\{F\}\+\_\+i = -\/ \textbackslash{}nabla \textbackslash{}sum\+\_\+\{\textbackslash{}substack\{j \textbackslash{}neq i\}\}$^\wedge$\{N\} \textbackslash{}phi\+\_\+\{ij\}./f\$ to calculate the force on particle i, where \textbackslash{}frac\{d \textbackslash{}phi\+\_\+\{ij\}\}\{dx\} = -\/24 \textbackslash{}epsilon x\+\_\+\{ij\} \textbackslash{}frac\{\textbackslash{}sigma$^\wedge$6\}\{r\+\_\+\{ij\}$^\wedge$8\} \textbackslash{}left( \textbackslash{}frac\{2\textbackslash{}sigma$^\wedge$6\}\{r\+\_\+\{ij\}$^\wedge$6\} -\/ 1 \textbackslash{}right). Using Newton\textquotesingle{}s Law, the force exerted on p\+\_\+i by p\+\_\+j is equal but opposite to the force exerted on p\+\_\+j by p\+\_\+i. Therefore, the total force is calculated on p\+\_\+i, but also the force exerted by p\+\_\+i on other particles.
